Shark Species Recognition Key

Interactive guide to identify shark species by visual characteristics

Great White Shark

Carcharodon carcharias
  • Size: 4-6 meters (13-20 ft)
  • Color: Grey top, white belly
  • Features: Triangular dorsal fin, black eyes
  • Habitat: Coastal waters, temperate
  • Behavior: Apex predator, solitary

Hammerhead Shark

Sphyrna mokarran
  • Size: 3-6 meters (10-20 ft)
  • Color: Grey-brown with lighter belly
  • Features: Distinctive hammer-shaped head
  • Habitat: Tropical and temperate waters
  • Behavior: School in large groups

Tiger Shark

Galeocerdo cuvier
  • Size: 2.4-3.3 meters (8-11 ft)
  • Color: Grey-blue with vertical stripes
  • Features: Blunt snout, striped markings
  • Habitat: Warm coastal waters
  • Behavior: Nocturnal, opportunistic feeders

Bull Shark

Carcharhinus leucas
  • Size: 2-2.4 meters (6.5-8 ft)
  • Color: Grey back, white underside
  • Features: Short snout, stocky body
  • Habitat: Coastal, freshwater rivers
  • Behavior: Aggressive, territorial

Lemon Shark

Negaprion brevirostris
  • Size: 0.9-2.4 meters (3-8 ft)
  • Color: Yellowish-brown body
  • Features: Flat head, small mouth
  • Habitat: Tropical/subtropical shallow waters
  • Behavior: Viviparous, live young

Whale Shark

Rhincodon typus
  • Size: 9-18 meters (30-59 ft) - Largest fish
  • Color: Grey with white spots
  • Features: Massive mouth, filter feeder
  • Habitat: Tropical waters
  • Behavior: Gentle, plankton feeder
